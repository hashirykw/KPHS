The academy

It started with
a few mats.

Revolution began as a small training centre with a handful of heavy bags and a founder who refused to water down the coaching. Zakir Abbas opened the doors so that people from any background could learn mixed martial arts properly — and stay long enough to get good at it.

Classes were small at the start. The energy wasn't. That room turned into two academies across Karachi, a fight team, and a steady stream of members who walked in for fitness and stayed for the sport.

Zakir Abbas

Founder & Head Coach

Your first 90 days

From walking in
to holding your own

1WEEK 1

Trial & fit

You watch, then you train. No fee, no kit needed — just clothes you can move in and a water bottle.

2WEEKS 2–4

Fundamentals

Stance, guard, base and breathing. The unglamorous month that decides whether everything after it works.

3WEEKS 5–8

Drilling live

Positional rounds with a partner and a coach watching. Resistance goes up only as fast as your control does.

4WEEKS 9–12

Rounds

Full sparring, controlled. By now you know what you're bad at — which is exactly when training gets interesting.
